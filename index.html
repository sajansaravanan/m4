Pillars of Creation

The Pillars of Creation are iconic structures located in the Eagle Nebula, about 6,500 light-years from Earth.

These towering columns of gas and dust are stellar nurseries where new stars are born.

The Hubble Space Telescope captured stunning images of these pillars, revealing intricate details.

The structures are composed primarily of hydrogen and are a testament to the ongoing process of star formation.

New observations continue to enhance our understanding of these fascinating cosmic features.
